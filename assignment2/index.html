The book that I choose to analyze for this text visualization is "A Bad Case of Stripes" by David Shannon. This children’s book sends a great message about the value of authenticity and the critical issues of social pressure around humans. I think this book will be a great text to visualize because of the message and the stunning colours that represent the vivid visual images in the book. Since it’s a book for children, this particular book can also relate to teenagers and adults from their early years of childhood development and personal issues, it provides a great frame for exploring acceptance and identity through programming. To give a brief summary of the story, it starts off with a young girl named Camilla Cream. She is very insecure about her personality and always anxious about what other people think of her. When she gets ready for her first day of school, she gets covered in rainbow stripes. She has attempted multiple ways to get rid of her stripes to no avail which caused her stripes to even grow further and get worse as per advice from her fellow family doctor. In the end of the story, her stripes eventually went away when she admitted that she likes lima beans in which an old lady visited her house for a “cure”. Her admission of liking lima beans was a suppression in which she fears that she may not be liked for it. Her retransformation conveys Camilla’s consciousness returning back to reality when she realizes the negative aspects of people-pleasing and other individual’s opinions. I choose this book in particular out of any other modern books because this book was a big part of my childhood in which I experience nostalgia from. Not only that, but it is also a very straightforward story for children to enjoy and to learn from. The way Camilla’s skin changes in the story makes a great opportunity for a graphical representation in the assignment. By using Three.Js, HTML, and CSS we can create a theme of acceptance and visualize different colours.

The first visualization of Camilla’s Text will change Camilla’s physical changes into a 3D Model in which each color and pattern creates a specific moment in the story. For example, we can create a visualization of Camilla transforming into her stripe appearance when she’s nervous which visualizes her fears with terms like (“stripes, beans, fear”). The UI will allow the viewers to interact with the timeline, they can observe how outside opinions influence her appearance, directly linking into how her pressures change her identity. Each part of the body could represent peer pressure, family expectations and her insecurities. In addition, the 3D Model is used in the visualization to depict Camilla’s overwhelming nature of her case. It shows how deeply external opinions can influence her mind affecting her reputation. The shifting patterns and colours are portrayed as a metaphor for the process of her transformation.
